Features

Everything a ride needs, on a screen you can actually read in direct sun — and none of it depends on a phone once it’s set up.

🛰️GPS & speed

Position, speed, heading, altitude, satellites and UTC time. L76K/CASIC and u-blox M10Q auto-detected, warm-started from the last-known fix.

🗺️Offline maps

1-bit H3 hexagonal vector tiles on the SD card. Zoom 1–8 m/px, follows GPS, optional track-up rotation. No data connection on the bike.

🧭GPX routes

Plan on the phone or drop a .gpx in /routes. The route draws on the map — ridden solid, ahead dashed — with km-remaining and turn banners.

⏺️Ride recording

1 Hz FIT files ready for Strava or intervals.icu. Moving time, avg/normalized power, HR and climbing. Rides cut short by a crash are repaired on next boot.

❤️BLE sensors

Heart rate, cycling power (with cadence from crank data) and speed/cadence. Pair once from the Sensors screen; pairings persist in flash.

⛰️Map-cached elevation

Altitude and climb read from a DEM grid baked into each map tile — not the GPS chip’s noisy barometric altitude.

⚙️On-device settings

Units, 12/24 h clock, FTP power zones, timezone, frontlight and USB-drive mode — persisted in NVS and mirrored to the companion app.

⬆️Easy updates

Flash from this page over USB, drop firmware.bin on the SD card, or push OTA from the app. A/B partitions protect the running image either way.

Phone / device split

The head unit is fully standalone. The iOS companion app is the authoring surface — anything awkward on a touch e-paper screen happens on the phone and is pushed over BLE.

🗺️ Generate an offline map

Pick an area, download a .ebm map, and drop it in /maps on the SD card — the device draws it offline, no phone needed. Everything runs locally in your browser from OpenStreetMap data (via Overpass); nothing is uploaded anywhere. This bakes a whole-region fallback map — the iOS app is still the way to author the per-hex tiles with baked-in elevation.

Pan and zoom to your area, then draw a box or use the current view.

⚡ Flash firmware from your browser

Powered by esptool-js and the Web Serial API — the same approach as the Adafruit WebSerial ESPTool. Everything runs locally in your browser; nothing is uploaded anywhere.

Your browser can’t do this. Web Serial needs a Chromium-based desktop browser — use Chrome, Edge or Opera on desktop (not Safari, Firefox or mobile).
1  Get a firmware binary

Grab firmware.bin from a CI build (the firmware artifact) or a release, or build it yourself with pio run.

2  Put the board in download mode

The board runs in USB-OTG mode, so it can’t auto-reset into the bootloader. Do it by hand: hold BOOT, tap RESET, then release BOOT. Then click Connect below and pick the usbmodem / USB Serial port.

3  Flash, then tap RESET to run

By default this writes just the app at 0x10000 — enough to update an already-set-up board. For a blank board or recovery, add the bootloader / partition rows under Advanced.

Advanced — full / blank-board flash

A full ESP32-S3 flash needs four parts. Add rows and supply each binary (from your PlatformIO .pio/build/t5s3-pro/ folder):

0x0bootloader.bin
0x8000partitions.bin
0xe000boot_app0.bin
0x10000firmware.bin
