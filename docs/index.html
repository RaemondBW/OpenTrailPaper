01 — Features

Everything a ride needs, nothing that phones home.

Fully standalone once set up — the phone is only for authoring maps, routes and settings.

GPS & speed

Position, speed, heading, altitude, satellites and UTC time. L76K/CASIC and u-blox M10Q auto-detected, warm-started from the last-known fix.

Offline maps

1-bit H3 hexagonal vector tiles on the SD card. Zoom 1–8 m/px, follows GPS, optional track-up rotation. No data connection on the bike.

GPX routes

Plan on the phone or drop a .gpx in /routes. The route draws on the map — ridden solid, ahead dashed — with km-remaining and turn banners.

Ride recording

1 Hz FIT files ready for Strava or intervals.icu. Moving time, avg/normalized power, HR and climbing. Rides cut short by a crash are repaired on next boot.

BLE sensors

Heart rate, cycling power (with cadence from crank data) and speed/cadence. Pair once from the Sensors screen; pairings persist in flash.

Real elevation

Altitude and climb read from a DEM grid baked into each map tile — not the GPS chip’s noisy barometric altitude.

On-device settings

Units, 12/24 h clock, FTP power zones, timezone, backlight and USB-drive mode — persisted in NVS and mirrored to the companion app.

Easy updates

Flash from this page over USB, drop firmware.bin on the SD card, or push OTA from the app. A/B partitions protect the running image either way.

02 — On the panel

Pixel-for-pixel real.

Every screenshot is rendered by the actual firmware drawing code (src/ui_render.cpp) compiled on the desktop — what you see is what the panel draws.

03 — Phone / device split

Standalone on the bike. Authored on the phone.

Anything awkward on a touch e-paper screen — typing a destination, drawing a map region, editing settings — happens in the iOS companion app and is pushed over BLE.

Device — ESP32-S3 firmware
  • GPS, sensors, ride recording, rendering, navigation
  • SD card storage — /rides /routes /maps /logs
  • Renders H3 tiles offline
  • Applies updates from USB, SD or BLE
iOS companion app
  • Map-tile authoring, route planning, ride history
  • Transient storage — everything streams over BLE
  • Fetches OSM + elevation, builds tiles
  • Bundles firmware.bin, pushes OTA

04 — Generate an offline map

Bake a map in your browser.

Pick an area, download a .ebm map, and drop it in /maps on the SD card — the device draws it offline, no phone needed. Everything runs locally in your browser from OpenStreetMap data (via Overpass); nothing is uploaded anywhere. This bakes a whole-region fallback map — the iOS app is still the way to author the per-hex tiles with baked-in elevation.

Pan and zoom to your area, then draw a box or use the current view.

1 Choose your area

Pan/zoom the map to where you ride, then Draw a box (drag across the map) or Use current view. Keep it modest — a city or a county is fine; a whole state is too much data for one download.

2 Generate
Pick an area to begin.

3 Unzip onto the SD card

Unzip the download onto the SD card’s root and it lands in /maps automatically — over the built-in USB-drive mode, or with a card reader. It shows up on the map screen the next time you ride through that area.

05 — Flash firmware

From zero to riding, in the browser.

Powered by esptool-js and the Web Serial API — everything runs locally in your browser; nothing is uploaded anywhere. Chrome or Edge on desktop.

Your browser can’t do this. Web Serial needs a Chromium-based desktop browser — use Chrome, Edge or Opera on desktop (not Safari, Firefox or mobile).
1 Get a firmware binary

Grab firmware.bin from a CI build (the firmware artifact) or a release, or build it yourself with pio run.

2 Put the board in download mode

The board runs in USB-OTG mode, so it can’t auto-reset into the bootloader. Do it by hand: hold BOOT, tap RESET, then release BOOT. Then click Connect and pick the usbmodem / USB Serial port.

3 Flash, then tap RESET to run

By default this writes just the app at 0x10000 — enough to update an already-set-up board. For a blank board or recovery, add the bootloader / partition rows under Advanced.

A full ESP32-S3 flash needs four parts. Add rows and supply each binary (from your PlatformIO .pio/build/t5s3-pro/ folder):

0x8000partitions.bin
